Google: The World's Most Popular Search Engine Google is an American multinational technology company that specializes in online advertising, search engine technology, cloud computing, computer software, quantum computing, e-commerce, consumer electronics, and artificial intelligence. It was founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin, who were Stanford University graduate students at the time.

Key Products and Services:

Google Search: The company's flagship product, it is the most widely used search engine on the internet. YouTube: The world's largest video-sharing platform, acquired by Google in 2006.

Gmail:

A free email service that is part of the Google Workspace suite.

Google Drive:

A cloud storage and file sharing service.

Google Maps:

A web-based mapping service that provides detailed maps, satellite imagery, street views, directions, and more.

Google Chrome:

A web browser developed by Google.

Android:

The most widely used mobile operating system, developed by Google.

Google Cloud Platform:

A cloud computing platform that offers a suite of cloud services.

Google's Mission:

Google's mission is to "organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful." The company strives to provide users with relevant and helpful information through its products and services.
